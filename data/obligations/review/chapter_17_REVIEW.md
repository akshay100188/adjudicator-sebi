# Review — Chapter 17: Early Warning Mechanism to prevent diversion of client securities

## Candidate obligations (19)

### SB-EWMDS-001 — Cooperate with Early Warning Mechanism Inspections – Submit Required Documents
- **Clauses:** 17.2.6, 17.2.6.I, 17.2.6.II
- **Category:** inspection_cooperation · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must cooperate during inspections conducted by Stock Exchanges and Depositories. This includes submitting all data sought during inspections (especially relating to bank/demat accounts and client ledgers) without repeated reminders, and providing reasonable access to records and office premises. Non-cooperation, as defined in the internal policy/guidelines framed by Stock Exchanges and Depositories, may trigger early warning actions.
- **Expected controls:** Documented inspection response procedure with designated responsible personnel; Centralised repository of bank/demat account records and client ledgers accessible for regulatory inspection; Policy for timely submission of inspection documents within prescribed timeframes; Physical/electronic access control procedures that permit regulator access to office premises and records

### SB-EWMDS-002 — Timely Reporting of Financial Health Data to Stock Exchanges/Depositories
- **Clauses:** 17.2.1, 17.2.1.c, 17.2.1.d
- **Category:** regulatory_reporting · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must not delay the reporting of its Annual Report, Balance Sheet, Internal Audit Reports, Risk Based Supervision (RBS) data, and any other data related to its financial health to the Stock Exchanges and/or Depositories. The stock broker must also submit, without failure, information sought by Stock Exchanges/Depositories regarding its dealings with related parties and promoters.
- **Expected controls:** Regulatory reporting calendar tracking all submission deadlines (Annual Report, Balance Sheet, Internal Audit, RBS data); Escalation process for timely preparation and submission of related-party dealing disclosures; Internal review checklist to verify completeness of submissions before the due date; Alert mechanism for impending submission deadlines with accountability assigned to a senior officer

### SB-EWMDS-003 — Monitor and Manage Net Worth and Financial Losses
- **Clauses:** 17.2.1, 17.2.1.a, 17.2.1.b
- **Category:** financial_health_monitoring · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must monitor its financial health, including net worth and profitability, to detect significant reductions in net worth over the previous half-year/year and significant losses in previous half-years/years, as these constitute early warning signal triggers that may prompt regulatory scrutiny and preventive action.
- **Expected controls:** Half-yearly and annual net worth computation and internal review process; Profit and loss monitoring against thresholds with escalation to senior management; Internal alert mechanism when net worth deterioration or significant losses are detected; Documentation of corrective measures taken when deterioration thresholds are breached

### SB-EWMDS-004 — Monitor and Control Mark-to-Market Losses on Proprietary and Related-Party Accounts
- **Clauses:** 17.2.1, 17.2.1.e, 17.2.1.g
- **Category:** proprietary_trading_risk · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must monitor significant mark-to-market (MTM) losses on its proprietary account and related-party accounts, and must monitor the extent of trading exposure, loans, advances, and investments made in related parties/group entities, as these are early warning signal parameters that may indicate financial deterioration and trigger regulatory action.
- **Expected controls:** Daily MTM computation for proprietary and related-party accounts with defined breach thresholds; Periodic review of loans, advances, and investments in related parties/group entities; Board/senior management reporting of related-party exposures breaching internal limits; Documented policy on permissible proprietary and related-party trading exposures

### SB-EWMDS-005 — Prevent and Monitor Repeated Pay-in Shortages
- **Clauses:** 17.2.1, 17.2.1.f
- **Category:** settlement_obligations · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must take measures to prevent repeated instances of pay-in shortages, as such occurrences constitute an early warning signal of financial deterioration and may trigger regulatory scrutiny and preventive action by Stock Exchanges.
- **Expected controls:** Pre-settlement monitoring of client and proprietary positions to ensure adequate securities for pay-in; Automated alerts for anticipated pay-in shortfalls prior to settlement deadlines; Root-cause analysis and corrective action process for each pay-in shortage occurrence; Internal reporting of pay-in shortage trends to compliance/risk management

### SB-EWMDS-006 — Monitor Dormant Account Activations and UCC Modifications
- **Clauses:** 17.2.1, 17.2.1.h, 17.2.1.i
- **Category:** client_account_monitoring · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must monitor and have controls over the sudden activation of a significant number of dormant client accounts and/or significant activity in dormant accounts, as well as a significant number of Unique Client Code (UCC) modifications, as these are early warning signals that may indicate misuse of client accounts.
- **Expected controls:** Automated alerts for sudden reactivation of multiple dormant client accounts within a defined period; Approval workflow and documentation requirements for UCC modifications; Threshold-based monitoring and reporting of dormant account activity to compliance; Periodic reconciliation of UCC modification records with client instructions

### SB-EWMDS-007 — Report and Respond to Changes in Key Personnel – Resignation of Statutory Auditors or Directors
- **Clauses:** 17.2.1, 17.2.1.j
- **Category:** governance_and_reporting · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must report and appropriately respond to the resignation of statutory auditors or directors, as such events constitute early warning signals of potential financial or governance deterioration and may trigger scrutiny from Stock Exchanges and Depositories.
- **Expected controls:** Documented procedure for immediate regulatory notification upon resignation of statutory auditors or directors; Board-level review of circumstances leading to auditor/director resignations; Timely appointment of replacement auditors/directors in accordance with applicable law; Record-keeping of all communications related to auditor/director resignations

### SB-EWMDS-008 — Upload Weekly Client Funds Monitoring Data Under Enhanced Supervision
- **Clauses:** 17.2.4, 17.2.4.c
- **Category:** regulatory_reporting · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must upload weekly data regarding monitoring of clients' funds as specified in SEBI's circular on Enhanced Supervision, without failure for three or more consecutive weeks, as non-compliance is an early warning signal trigger that may result in regulatory action.
- **Expected controls:** Weekly upload schedule for client funds monitoring data with automated reminders; Checklist and sign-off process confirming successful data upload each week; Escalation procedure when upload failures are detected; Record-keeping of upload confirmations and any exceptions with explanations

### SB-EWMDS-009 — Ensure Accuracy of Client Funds and Securities Reporting – Avoid Mis-reporting
- **Clauses:** 17.2.4, 17.2.4.e
- **Category:** regulatory_reporting · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must ensure that all reporting related to client funds and securities is accurate and must not mis-report or wrongly report data on client funds/securities in submissions made under Risk Based Supervision (RBS) or Enhanced Supervision, as mis-reporting is an early warning signal that may trigger regulatory action.
- **Expected controls:** Independent review/checker process for client funds and securities data before submission; Reconciliation of RBS/Enhanced Supervision data against internal books and records prior to upload; Written attestation by responsible senior officer confirming accuracy of submissions; Audit trail and documentation of data sources used for regulatory submissions

### SB-EWMDS-010 — Prohibit Pledging of Securities of Credit-Balance Clients for Own/Other Purposes
- **Clauses:** 17.2.4, 17.2.4.d
- **Category:** client_funds_settlement · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must not pledge securities of clients who have a credit balance and use the funds so raised for the stock broker's own purposes or for funding the debit balance of other clients, as this constitutes an early warning signal and indicator of misuse of client assets.
- **Expected controls:** System-level controls preventing pledge of securities belonging to credit-balance clients; Regular reconciliation to detect any pledge of credit-balance client securities; Policy prohibiting use of client pledged funds for proprietary or third-party client purposes; Periodic internal audit review of pledge transactions against client account balances

### SB-EWMDS-011 — Recover Dues from Debit-Balance Clients and Settle Dues to Credit-Balance Clients Promptly
- **Clauses:** 17.2.4, 17.2.4.a, 17.2.4.b
- **Category:** client_funds_settlement · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must have processes to prevent non-recovery of significant dues from debit-balance clients over a period of time and must not carry significant outstanding dues to credit-balance clients over a period of time, as both conditions are early warning signals under Risk Based Supervision (RBS) monitoring.
- **Expected controls:** Ageing analysis of debit-balance client dues with escalation and recovery procedures; Regular reporting and resolution process for significant credit-balance client dues; Threshold-based alerts for persistent debit/credit balance positions beyond defined periods; Internal audit review of client ledger balances at defined intervals

### SB-EWMDS-012 — Avoid Prolonged Terminal Disablement Across Segments or Exchanges
- **Clauses:** 17.2.5
- **Category:** operational_continuity · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must ensure that its trading terminals are not disabled for a significant number of days in any segment or Stock Exchange in the previous quarter, as terminal disablement is an early warning signal that may trigger regulatory scrutiny and preventive actions.
- **Expected controls:** Monitoring of trading terminal status across all segments and exchanges with documented uptime records; Escalation and resolution procedure for terminal disablement incidents; Root-cause analysis and corrective action for recurring terminal disablements; Internal compliance review of terminal operational status on a quarterly basis

### SB-EWMDS-013 — Promptly Address Investor Complaints Relating to Unauthorized Trading and Non-Receipt of Funds/Securities
- **Clauses:** 17.2.3
- **Category:** investor_grievance_redressal · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must resolve investor complaints alleging unauthorized trading, unauthorized delivery instructions, and non-receipt of funds and securities in a timely manner. An increase in the number of unresolved such complaints is an early warning signal that may trigger preventive regulatory action.
- **Expected controls:** Complaint tracking system with categorisation for unauthorized trading and funds/securities complaints; Defined turnaround times and escalation path for resolution of investor complaints; Periodic management review of pending and unresolved investor complaints; Root-cause analysis for recurring complaint categories with documented corrective action

### SB-EWMDS-014 — Maintain Accurate Securities Register for Pay-in/Pay-out Against Settlement
- **Clauses:** 17.6.1.2
- **Category:** securities_recordkeeping · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must maintain an accurate securities register recording all securities received and transferred against pay-in/pay-out related to settlement, enabling Stock Exchanges to verify the same in the event early warning signals are triggered.
- **Expected controls:** Maintenance of a securities register recording all inward/outward movement of securities against settlement obligations; Daily reconciliation of securities register with depository account statements and clearing corporation records; Access controls ensuring integrity of securities register data; Periodic internal audit review of securities register accuracy

### SB-EWMDS-015 — Comply with Shortened Running Account Settlement Timelines When Prescribed
- **Clauses:** 17.6.1.5
- **Category:** client_funds_settlement · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** When Stock Exchanges prescribe and monitor shorter time durations for the settlement of running accounts of clients (as a preventive action following early warning signal analysis), a stock broker must comply with the prescribed shorter settlement timelines for client running accounts.
- **Expected controls:** Monitoring system to track running account settlement timelines per client; Operational process to execute running account settlements within any shortened timelines directed by Stock Exchanges; Compliance confirmation mechanism for each running account settlement cycle; Record-keeping of all running account settlements with dates and amounts

### SB-EWMDS-016 — Cooperate with Stock Exchange Meetings Seeking Explanation on Financial Health
- **Clauses:** 17.6.1.6
- **Category:** regulatory_cooperation · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** When a Stock Exchange conducts a meeting with the designated directors of a stock broker to seek explanation regarding deteriorating financial health or suspected diversion of client funds/securities, the stock broker's designated directors must cooperate and provide appropriate explanation.
- **Expected controls:** Designation of directors responsible for attending regulatory inquiry meetings; Internal briefing process to prepare designated directors with accurate and documented responses; Record-keeping of all communications and minutes from meetings with regulators/exchanges; Legal/compliance review prior to regulatory meetings involving financial health inquiries

### SB-EWMDS-017 — Comply with Trading Terminal Deactivation Orders from Stock Exchanges
- **Clauses:** 17.6.1.7
- **Category:** regulatory_compliance_action · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** A stock broker must comply with uniform deactivation of its trading terminals by all Stock Exchanges, where such deactivation is based on communication received from another Stock Exchange as part of preventive action following early warning signal analysis.
- **Expected controls:** Documented procedure for immediate compliance with trading terminal deactivation orders; Communication protocol to all relevant internal teams upon receipt of deactivation instruction; Confirmation process to the issuing Stock Exchange that terminals have been deactivated; Record-keeping of all deactivation orders received and corresponding compliance actions

### SB-EWMDS-018 — Cooperate with Forensic Audit When Client Money and Securities Diversion is Suspected
- **Clauses:** 17.6.1.10
- **Category:** inspection_cooperation · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** Where diversion of client money and securities is suspected, a stock broker must cooperate with a forensic auditor appointed by the Stock Exchange to trace the entire trail of client funds and securities, providing access to all relevant records and information.
- **Expected controls:** Policy requiring full cooperation with forensic auditors including provision of bank, demat, and client records; Centralised, auditable records of all client fund and securities movements; Designated internal coordinator for managing forensic audit information requests; Legal/compliance review process for forensic audit cooperation procedures

### SB-EWMDS-019 — Comply with Restrictions on Use of Client Power of Attorney to Settlement Obligations Only
- **Clauses:** 17.6.2.2
- **Category:** client_securities_safeguarding · **Intermediary:** trading_member
- **Obligation:** When directed by a Depository as a preventive action, a stock broker must restrict the use of Power of Attorney (POA) granted by clients to the stock broker solely to meet the settlement obligation of the respective client. Off-market transfers must be executed only through client-issued electronic instructions or Delivery Instruction Slips (DIS), not through POA.
- **Expected controls:** System controls limiting POA usage to settlement obligations only when directed by Depository; Process to obtain client-issued electronic instructions or DIS for all off-market transfers; Audit trail of all POA invocations with mapping to specific settlement obligations; Training for operations staff on restricted POA usage requirements
